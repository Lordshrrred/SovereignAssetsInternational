The GMRWS Architecture

Five Pillars. One Integrated Platform.

Each pillar is independently deployable and collectively self-reinforcing ~ mercury capture protects the water, water infrastructure sustains the community, and soil restoration returns the land.

A

Pillar A

Mercury Capture

Proprietary hermetic containment systems achieving >99% capture efficiency ~ eliminating the primary toxin in artisanal mining environments and protecting watersheds for generations.

B

Pillar B

Community Water Infrastructure

Solar-powered pumping systems, distribution networks, and village-scale storage delivering potable water to communities of 200-5,000 people with 20+ year operational lifespans.

C

Pillar C

Subsurface Intelligence

Hyper-Spectral Plasma Detection fused with satellite imagery and AI ~ 3D environmental mapping to 50m depth for aquifer identification and contamination plume assessment.

D

Pillar D

Trident System

Molecular stabilization technology that transforms toxic mine tailings into fertile agricultural substrate ~ reclaiming land and livelihoods for mining-affected communities.

E

Pillar E

Hydrogeological Infrastructure

Precision aquifer mapping, source protection protocols, and basin-wide hydrological monitoring ~ defending primary water systems from surface contamination.
